Set 4 — New Urban Business Ideas

10 fresh business concepts for a sustainable smart city — Prepared by Prajwal Biradar

Smart Waste-to-Energy Plant

SDG 11.6 — Clean Cities / Energy
What it does
  • Converts municipal solid waste into electricity and biogas via controlled combustion/anaerobic digestion.
  • Reduces landfill load and generates renewable energy for streetlights, pumping stations, or micro-grids.
Why needed
  • Tackles growing garbage volumes while producing clean power and reducing methane emissions.
Revenue: sale of electricity/biogas, tipping fees, carbon credits.

Drone Delivery & Aerial Mapping Service

SDG 11.2 & 11.A — Mobility & Urban Ops
What it does
  • Fast drone delivery for medicines, urgent parcels; aerial mapping for construction and traffic monitoring.
Why needed
  • Speeds up critical deliveries and provides high-resolution data for planners at low marginal cost.
Revenue: delivery fees, mapping contracts, subscription for data access.

Water ATM & Purification Kiosk

SDG 11.1 — Basic Services & Health
What it does
  • 24/7 kiosks dispensing treated drinking water (RO+UV) with smart card or mobile payments.
Why needed
  • Ensures affordable clean water, reduces plastic bottle waste, improves public health.
Revenue: pay-per-litre charges, subscription cards, municipal partnerships.

City Cybersecurity & Data Protection Agency

SDG 11.3 — Resilient Digital Infrastructure
What it does
  • Secures municipal networks, IoT devices, and citizen data; runs audits and incident response.
Why needed
  • Smart-city systems are high-value targets—data protection is essential for trust and continuity.
Revenue: service contracts with city departments and private operators.

Urban Mobility App (All-in-One)

SDG 11.2 — Integrated Transport
What it does
  • Combines bus schedules, metro, bike rentals, auto booking, parking, and route planning in one app.
Why needed
  • Streamlines travel decisions, reduces waiting times and improves public transport usage.
Revenue: ads, premium features, partnerships with operators.

Smart Building Automation & IoT Service

SDG 11.3 — Efficient Buildings
What it does
  • Installs automation for HVAC, lighting, access control, metering, and fire safety across buildings.
Why needed
  • Reduces operational cost and energy usage, improves occupant comfort and safety.
Revenue: installation fees, subscriptions for monitoring & maintenance.

Public Charging Kiosks for Mobiles & Laptops

SDG 11.1 — Accessible Services
What it does
  • Solar-powered & secure charging booths (lockers, USB, wireless) at transit hubs, markets, and campuses.
Why needed
  • Provides essential convenience for citizens and travellers; supports digital inclusion.
Revenue: pay-per-use, ads on kiosk screens, sponsorships.

Smart Pet Care & Animal Tracking Service

SDG 11.7 — Inclusive Community Services
What it does
  • GPS collars, vaccination & health reminders, lost-pet alerts, grooming and vet-on-demand services.
Why needed
  • Rising pet ownership needs organized services for health and safety of animals and citizens.
Revenue: device sales, subscription tracking, clinic commissions.

Skill Training & Innovation Lab (AI / Robotics)

SDG 11.3 & 11.A — Jobs & Education
What it does
  • Hands-on training in AI, robotics, 3D printing, drones, and digital skills for youth and workers.
Why needed
  • Builds workforce readiness and creates local tech entrepreneurship opportunities.
Revenue: course fees, corporate training contracts, incubation services.

Eco-Friendly Packaging & Recycling Store

SDG 11.6 — Waste Reduction
What it does
  • Manufactures biodegradable packaging; runs take-back programs where customers return plastics for discounts.
Why needed
  • Reduces single-use plastic usage and supports circular-economy initiatives.
Revenue: product sales, B2B supply contracts, recycling incentives.
